A Tribute to Nelson Mandela

A champion for justice and equality, Nelson Mandela was a South African revolutionary who fought against racial segregation (apartheid). After 27 years of imprisonment for his activism, Mandela emerged as the nation's first black president (1994-1999), leading the country towards peace and reconciliation. Recognized for his unwavering spirit, he was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1993, becoming a global symbol of forgiveness and human dignity.
